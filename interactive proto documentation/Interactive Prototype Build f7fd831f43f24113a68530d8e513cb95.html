Version 2

Prototype

Playtest:

Conducted in three rounds →

  1. Anna and Jasmine (Team 1)
  1. Apurv and Leo (Team 2)
  1. Krithi Nalla, my cousin and I (Team 3)

Biggest questions →

  1. Was the game too prescriptive or could people play it in many different ways
  1. Is the difficulty level significant → the game should neither be impossible nor easy as they both send the wrong message. Rather, it should be achievable and challenging
  1. Checking player interactions + communication + work together
  1. Role-based game play
  1. Working with randomised board
  1. How was the onboarding process

Observations:

  1. Everyone played the game very differently. Team 1 focussed on the grass and finding the best places to build. The dispersed their houses across the board.

    Team 2 was all about efficiency of packing buildings together.

    Team 3 was similar to Team 2 but we discovered a way to plant trees just before water levels rise so as to lose carbon without losing space.

  1. The difficulty level was definitely on the right level →

    Out of the three games, one went smoothly and the team manages to win. However, the other two games hd players losing with 2-3 moves left.

    This made the experience a bit frustrating but not demotivating.

    I know from personal experience I immediately wanted to dive right back into the game to figure it out again

  1. Players were overall able to communicate and make changes really easily.
  1. Role based aspect of the game was inherently unnecessary. The game was initially designed so one person removed pieces and the other placed new ones. However, in a board game setting this isn’t enforced. So ultimately it became a game where players would just do everything together.
  1. Randomised board → I loved working with the randomised boards but there was two easily spotted mistakes I made
    1. I didn’t take a picture of the board at the beginning of any of these games to compare how the initial game state varied across all three
    1. I didn’t decide how many trees and soil would be on the board. I suspect this difference in the ratio of grass: trees resulted in sone of the game hoards being much harder than others.
  1. Rules still required some tweaking. But as people played the game, they definitely understood how it worked.

Player comments:
